The case law of our Court and of the Federal Court of Appeal in immigration matters is to the effect that when the absence of counsel results in depriving the individual of his right to a fair hearing, the decision is invalid (see
Castroman v. Canada (Secretary of State)
, [1994] F.C.J. No. 962; 81 F.T.R. 227 (T.D.);
Nemeth
, supra;
McCarthy v. Minister of Employment and Immigration
, [1979] 1 F.C. 121; 22 N.R. 306 (F.C.A.);
Gargano v. Canada (Minister of Citizenship and Immigration)
, [1994] F.C.J. No. 1385; 85 F.T.R. 49 (T.D.);
De Sousa v. Minister of Employment and Immigration
, [1988] F.C.J. No. 569; 93 N.R. 31 (F.C.A.)).
