  The general rule in respect of PRRA decisions is that they are subject to the standard of review of reasonableness ( 
 Aleziri v. Canada (Minister of Citizenship and Immigration) 
 , [2009] F.T.R. Uned. 24; 2009 FC 38). However, the Applicant argues that she was entitled to an oral hearing because there was an adverse credibility finding, and that there was a breach of procedural fairness, issues assessed on a standard of correctness.
