Therefore, while
Haghighi
held that the PCDO's risk assessment should have been disclosed to the applicant even though it contained no facts, or allegations of fact, that were not within the knowledge of the applicant (by virtue of being publicly available to him), this holding must be limited to the facts upon which it was based. Disclosure was required, in the Court's view, because immigration officials are likely to give "decisive weight" to the opinions of PCDOs as a result of their relative expertise in assessing risk.
