The focus was on whether information on country conditions constituted extrinsic evidence. The Court concluded that having regard to the minimal duty of fairness according to
Shah v. Minister of Employment and Immigration
, [1994] F.C.J. No. 1299; 170 N.R. 238 (F.C.A.), unless the country conditions information is not public and is material to the decision, the PCDO's failure to disclose the material prior to reaching a decision did not amount to a breach of the rules of procedural fairness.
