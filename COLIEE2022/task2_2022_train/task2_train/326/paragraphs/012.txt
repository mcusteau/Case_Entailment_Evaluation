The last regular meeting of the I.T.U.M. Council was held on May 25, 2004, that is, two weeks prior to the start of the electoral campaign on June 8. Following the July 8 elections, Chief Rosario Pinette was replaced by Elie-Jacques Jourdain. Five councillors who supported the former chief were re-elected (Céline Bellefleur Vollant, Georges-Ernest Grégoire, Gilles Jourdain, Ronald Fontaine, Marcelle St-Onge), while four councillors supported the new chief (Marie-Marthe Fontaine, Bernadette Michel, Yves Rock and the applicant, Albert Vollant).
