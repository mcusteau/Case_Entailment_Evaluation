  The Board's interpretation of the Act will be reviewed on a correctness standard, while the Board's application of the law to the facts will be considered on a standard of reasonableness ( 
 Dunsmuir 
  at paragraph 164). Reasonableness will also be used to consider whether the Board erred in making its finding of credibility: Aguirre v. Canada (Minister of Citizenship and Immigration), 2008 FC 571.
