To be clearly descriptive, a mark must be more than merely suggestive of the character or quality of the wares or services with which it is associated; its descriptive character must go to the material composition, or intrinsic quality of the goods or services. The test is one of immediate impression. (
Unitel Communications Inc. v. Bell Canada
(1995), 92 F.T.R. 161; 61 C.P.R.(3d) 12 at 55 (T.D.)).
