As stated by Wetston, J., in

Rahman and

"The central issue to be determined by the Board is whether the change is sufficient in terms of its effect on the social and political climate of the country of origin, given the basis of the fear on which the claimant relies:
'Whether a change of circumstances is sufficient for a fear of persecution to be no longer well-founded must naturally be determined in relation to the basis of and reasons for the fear relied on.'"
