The principal female plaintiff, Jeannine Hakizimana, alleged a well-founded fear of persecution in her country of origin, Rwanda, on account of her race, namely her membership in the Hutu ethnic group, and her alleged political opinions. Her minor children, Astrid Bicamumpaka Shema ("Astrid") and Cédric Bicamumpaka ("Cédric"), based their claims on hers. Astrid is a national of Rwanda and Cédric has dual Rwandan and Belgian nationality.
