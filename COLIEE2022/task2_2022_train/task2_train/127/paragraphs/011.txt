  The applicant therefore had the burden of proving with clear and convincing evidence, on a balance of probabilities, that the said protection was inadequate or non-existent. She was required to demonstrate that she had offered her country the real possibility of intervening before being able to legitimately infer that it was not capable of providing her with the necessary protection ( 
 Hinzman et al v Canada (The Minister of Citizenship and Immigration) 
 , 2007 FCA 171, 362 NR 1).
