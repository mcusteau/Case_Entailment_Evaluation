  The evidence in support of the application for the order was furnished by Jeffrey Spencer, Director, Marketing Planning of Merck Frosst Canada & Co. He swore that the market for the treatment and prevention of osteoporosis is competitive and expressed concern that if Merck's competitors were given access to confidential information it could advantage them in the market place. He had been informed by his solicitors that material normally filed in Court becomes part of the public record, and was advised by Merck's U.S. attorney that a protective order in similar form had been issued in the United States with respect to the same types of documents.
