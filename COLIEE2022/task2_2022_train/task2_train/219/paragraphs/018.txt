  It is well settled that the purpose of an assessment of the risks of returning is not to give applicants a right to appeal the RPD's decision or to have the evidence reassessed by the PRRA officer or by this Court. The RPD's findings of fact therefore became  
 res judicata 
  once the Court dismissed the applicants' leave application challenging the RPD's decision ( 
 Danyluk v. Ainsworth Technologies Inc 
 ., [2001] 2 S.C.R. 460, at paragraph 24;  
 Angle v. Canada (Minister of National Revenue - M.N.R.) 
 , [1975] 2 S.C.R. 248, at page 254).
