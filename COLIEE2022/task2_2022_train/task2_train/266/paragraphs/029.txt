Here the Board made an inference, based only on the documents submitted by the Minister, that Sanchez must have had knowledge, despite his denials. Given the gravity of the conclusion that, in having such knowledge, Sanchez was "complicit" in the atrocity and therefore "committed" them, the Board should have relied upon more than inference in light of Sanchez's denials. It should have considered seriously the statements in the Minister's own documents put in evidence as to the heavy censoring of the media.
