In its analysis, the Board considered only one issue - whether the Applicants' fear of persecution was related to any of the grounds enumerated in the Convention refugee definition in s. 2 of the
Immigration Act
. The Board found that the Applicants were the targets of a private vendetta - vengeance by the wife's family because the Applicants married against their wishes and that fear of persecution from the wife's family was not related to any of the grounds enumerated in the Convention refugee definition.
