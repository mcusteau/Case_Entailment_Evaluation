  Finally, the Applicant also seeks costs. Rule 22 of the  
 Federal Courts Immigration and Refugee Protection Rules 
 , SOR/93-22 prohibits any award of costs in this application for judicial review "unless the Court, for special reasons, so orders". The threshold for "special reasons" is high ( 
 Uppal v. Canada (Minister of Citizenship and Immigration) 
 , [2005] F.T.R. Uned. A61; 141 A.C.W.S.(3d) 831; 2005 FC 1133) and, upon reviewing the record, the Court concludes there exists no special reasons justifying costs in this matter.
