Elmer John Trotter served in the RCAF in the United Kingdom, flying on Lancaster Bombers as a Special Duties Pilot and as a Pathfinder. On his 44th mission, he was shot down over Germany and was captured. He spent 268 days as a prisoner of war. During that time, he was extensively interrogated. He was twice accused of being a spy and was placed before a firing squad, but not executed.
