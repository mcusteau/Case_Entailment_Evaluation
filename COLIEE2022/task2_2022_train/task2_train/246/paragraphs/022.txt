Mr. Marr says that the basis for the special circumstances, as set out in s. 78(4), is to ensure consideration of the highest level of educational achievement, and notes that the Court has previously held that in Scotland, the two-year credential following 11 years of secondary education triggers the application of s. 78(4), resulting in the award of 20 points under the education category:
McLachlan v. Canada (Minister of Citizenship and Immigration)
, 2009 FC 975. Accordingly, he submits that the officer's award of 15 points for education was an error of law.
