  The applicant submits that in keeping with Ward, supra, the issue in the test for determining the availability of state protection is not whether the state "makes efforts" to protect its citizenry as stated by the PRRA Officer. It is whether it was objectively reasonable for the applicant not to have sought the protection of the state. The applicant submits that in light of his personal experiences, the experiences of similarly situated persons and the extensive documentary evidence about police involvement in the persecution of Hungarian Roma, it was reasonable to not have sought the protection of the state.
