  Moreover, it was not reasonable for the RAD to conclude that the applicant should have brought the documentary evidence before the RPD. The document was not in his possession, and he mistakenly believed that CBSA still had it, based on the fact that the agency had seized it from him initially. The RAD seemingly recognized that the failure to produce the document was the fault of the lawyer. The CBSA had faxed it to the lawyer after the RPD hearing and the lawyer had failed to forward it to the applicant before the RAD determination.
