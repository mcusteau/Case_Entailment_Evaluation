 [31] 
  The onus of proof on the party moving to strike is a heavy one. The discretion to strike out pleadings should only be exercised in plain and obvious cases where the Court is satisfied beyond doubt that the allegation cannot be supported and is certain to fail at trial because it contains a radical defect: Hunt v. Carey Canada Ltd., [1990] 2 S.C.R. 959, (1990), 74 D.L.R. (4th) 321.
