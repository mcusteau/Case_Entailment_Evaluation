  The facts in  
 Andreoli v. Canada (M.C.I.) 
 , [2004] F.C.J. No. 1349, 2004 FC 111, are not unlike those before me. In  
 Andreoli 
 , as here, the applicants were not themselves negligent but instead had trusted a representative who bore all responsibility for the procedural error. Justice Harrington, after citing  
 Ahamad 
 , supra, wrote at paragraph 16: 
 
 
 
 In order to assess a case such as this, it is absolutely paramount to opt for a contextual approach and to avoid the mire of procedural dogma. I refer to the words of the Honourable Mr. Justice Pigeon in  
 Hamel v. Brunette 
 , [1977] 1 S.C.R. 147, 156, where he very aptly wrote that "procedure [should] be the servant of justice and not its mistress".
