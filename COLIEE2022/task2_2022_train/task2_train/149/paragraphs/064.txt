  There is little doubt that in order to better carry out its mandate, the RPD should keep abreast of international developments and would be well advised to consider this documentation as well as recent decisions of other immigration tribunals. For example, the decision of the English Immigration Appeal Tribunal in Gurung v. The Secretary of State for the Home Department, [2002] UKAIT 04870 provides excellent information as to how a decision-maker should approach a case involving subsection 1F) in the Refugee Convention.
