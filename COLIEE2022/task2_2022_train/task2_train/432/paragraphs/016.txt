Every court of review was also reminded by the Supreme Court of Canada in
Boulis v. Canada (Minister of Manpower and Immigration)
, [1974] S.C.R. 875, at 885, that decisions by administrative boards should not be analyzed or parsed microscopically as if one were dealing with a matter of statute interpretation. As stated by Joyal, J., in
Matsushita Electric Industrial Co. v. Panavision Inc.
(1992), 53 F.T.R. 228; 40 C.P.R.(3d) 486, at 493 (T.D.) [C.P.R.]:
"... such decisions must be read as a whole to give them an over-all meaning undeterred by a certain lack of precision in the language used, or by the occasional ambiguity of expression ..."
