  In addition, it has been determined that in exercising a discretion conferred by section 4.8 of the  
 Aeronautics Act 
  "the Minister may take any factor he considers relevant into account". See  
 Fontaine v Canada 
 , 2007 FC 1160 at para 78, where the Court also concluded that, where safety is an issue of substantial importance, access to restricted areas is a privilege, not a right. The factors which can be considered include criminal charges which do not result in a conviction; the fact that charges are stayed has been found not to be determinative. See  
 Thep-Outhainthany v Canada (AG) 
 , 2013 FC 59 at para 19.
