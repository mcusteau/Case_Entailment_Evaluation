One must start with the proposition that, in Canada's federal system, the superior courts of provinces have plenary and inherent jurisdiction to hear and decide all cases that come before them, regardless of whether the law applicable to a particular case is provincial, federal or constitutional (
Ordon et al. v. Grail
, [1998] 3 S.C.R. 437; 232 N.R. 201; 115 O.A.C. 1; 166 D.L.R.(4th) 193 per Iacobucci and Major, JJ., for the Court at paragraph 44). Jurisdiction of the Federal Court over a matter cannot be presumed, it must be positively demonstrated (
Blacktop (R.W.) Ltd. et al. v. Artec Equipment Co. et al.
(1991), 50 F.T.R. 225; 39 C.P.R.(3d) 432 (T.D.) per Rouleau, J., at 435).
