At page 438, Dickson, J., summarized a three step process of analysis:
(1) First, the court must determine whether the impugned provision can be viewed as intruding on provincial powers and, if so, to what extent;
(2) Second, the court must establish whether the
Act
, or severable part, is valid as forming part is a regulatory scheme falling under federal competence; and
(3) Third, is the impugned provision sufficiently integrated into the regulatory scheme.
