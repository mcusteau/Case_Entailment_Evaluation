  The applicant submits that the Board erred in its assessment of state protection by failing to mention or address negative evidence pointing to the lack of state protection for abused women in Mexico. The Board listed various measures in place to assist women and victims of violence; however, the only acknowledgement of contradictory evidence is as follows: 
 
 
 
 Documentary evidence acknowledges crime, corruption and a prevalence of domestic abuse in Mexico, but it also states that the government is taking steps to address these issues. As Mexico is a democracy, the presumption of state protection applies.3 
 
 
 
 ... 
 
 
 
 The panel recognizes that domestic abuse of women is a serious problem in Mexico. The documentary evidence, however, shows that Mexico is making serious efforts to address this problem.
