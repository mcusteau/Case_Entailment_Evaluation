  The Board's determination with regard to the availability of state protection is reviewable on the basis of patent unreasonableness. It is entitled to great deference and will only be set aside if patently unreasonable,  
 Quijano v. Canada (Minister of Citizenship and Immigration) 
 , 2005 FC 1706. Once these findings are made, they are assessed as a question of mixed fact and law, i.e. a standard of reasonableness  
 simpliciter 
 , see  
 Chavez v. Canada (Minister of Citizenship and Immigration) 
 , 2005 FC 193 at para 11;  
 Monte Rey Nunez v. Canada (Minister of Citizenship and Immigration) 
 , 2005 FC 1661.
