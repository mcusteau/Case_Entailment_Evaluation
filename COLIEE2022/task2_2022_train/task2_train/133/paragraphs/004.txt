  In his reasons for decision, the Chairman of the Appeal Board found that there was prima facie evidence that the personal suitability of the candidates had been assessed. Although he found it "surprising" that the candidates received identical ratings on all of the sub-factors and "unfortunate" that the analysis was not described in the documentation prepared by the Selection Board, he concluded as follows: 
 
 
 
 "However, I cannot set aside the Selection Board's findings without any information to show that the marks awarded did not fairly reflect the level of the candidates' personal qualities. No information has been brought forward to show why the personal qualities of the appellant should have been rated any differently than the personal qualities of the selected candidate. While the assessments made by the Selection Board may seem unusual, there is no reason for me to conclude that they are inaccurate."
