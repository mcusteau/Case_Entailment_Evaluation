In any event, the Refugee Division's credibility finding was also supported by the principal applicant's limited knowledge of the Jewish faith and the omission of significant facts from his PIF. These concerns were raised by the panel at the hearing and the applicants were given a chance to address them, but their responses did no satisfy the panel. Even if the principal applicant's birth certificate was authentic, these shortcomings are sufficient to support the panel's negative credibility finding.
