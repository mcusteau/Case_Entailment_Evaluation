The role of the Commission has been authoritatively determined by the Supreme Court of Canada in
Cooper v. Canadian Human Rights Commission
, [1996] 3 S.C.R. 854; 204 N.R. 1. In
Cooper
, that court had an opportunity to consider and build upon two of its previous judgments analyzing the functions of the Commission, namely
Syndicat des employés de production du Québec et de l'Acadie v. Commission canadienne des droits de la personne et al.
, [1989] 2 S.C.R. 879; 100 N.R. 241 and
Canada (Attorney General) v. Mossop
, [1993] 1 S.C.R. 554; 149 N.R. 1.
