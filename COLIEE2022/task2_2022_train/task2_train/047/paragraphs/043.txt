  The applicant submits that the Appeal Board erred in law by refusing to accept into evidence a complete copy of the Krupp report. The applicant argues that the conclusions of the Krupp report support his view that individuals suffering from MS are at a significant disadvantage if given a written exam containing true/false or multiple-choice questions. The respondent contends that as the Appeal Board Chairperson specifically accepted into evidence and considered a summary of the Krupp report, the applicant cannot argue the rules of natural justice were breached.
