The applicant states that after he was escorted aboard the plane, the immigration officers gave his passport to the stewardess, then left. He maintains that before the flight took off, the stewardess permitted him to return to the waiting room to look for his briefcase, that there was no sign of any immigration people anywhere and that before he found his briefcase, the flight took off without him. I find the scenario difficult to believe, but for the purposes of this decision, the apprehension and deportation are of greater significance.
