  The Superior Court then goes through some of the jurisprudence above referred to, including my own decision in Belliveau v. The Queen, [1984] 2 F.C. 384; 13 C.C.C.(3d) 138 where I said as follows, as reported in the MacAllister judgment (at page 8): 
 
 
 
 "It is not unrealistic to assume that some form of control and rehabilitation is indicated to assist prisoners in their gradual re-entry into the community and that some type of safeguard is warranted for the protection of that community. Mandatory supervision is one method for achieving those goals and the limitations it imposes are reasonable and justifiable in a democratic society."
