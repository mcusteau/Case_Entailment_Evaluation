  The petitioner submits that it is prima facie evident that the special condition clearly infringes on those two constitutional rights which he could lawfully exercise, enjoy and pursue to the full extent not incompatible with the terms and conditions governing his parole. Thus, up to the imposition of the condition, the petitioner could associate, socialize and converse with any individual and could pursue the gaining of a livelihood without restrictions or qualification, so long as those rights were exercised in accordance with the law.
