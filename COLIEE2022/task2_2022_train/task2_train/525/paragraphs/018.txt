In other cases, the Federal Court has noted that the employer's stated intention is not determinative, and adjudicators may have to consider whether what is apparently an administrative action is in actual fact "disguised discipline". This involves an assessment of all the surrounding facts and circumstances (see, e.g.,
Canada (Attorney General) v. Grover
