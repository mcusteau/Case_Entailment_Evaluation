For these reasons I conclude that the scope of discretion available to the Applicants in these cases is no greater than that described in Cha, above, which is to say that aggravating and disputed mitigating circumstances are effectively off the table.  It is open to the Officer and the Delegate to reflect on “clear and non-controversial” facts concerning the grounds of inadmissibility – and presumably to entertain a submission about those facts – but the legal obligation extends no further than that.
