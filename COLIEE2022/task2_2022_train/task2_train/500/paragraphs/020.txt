In order for the Applicant to succeed, she must satisfy all three branches of the test developed by the Federal Court of Appeal in
Toth v. Minister of Employment and Immigration
(1988), 86 N.R. 302 (F.C.A.). In other words, the Court must be satisfied that the Applicant has raised a serious issue, that she will suffer irreparable harm if removed from Canada, and that the balance of convenience favours her.
