The respondent further submits that the fact that RPOs and Board members may question claimants does not make the process adversarial. Thorough questioning has been recognized by the Court as consistent with the Board's mandate to get at the truth of claims. If a member goes too far, an effective remedy is always available to claimants through judicial review:
Cota v. Canada (Ministre de la Citoyenneté et de l'Immigration)
, [1999] F.T.R. Uned. 350; [1999] F.C.J. No. 872 (T.D.), at para. 26;
Sindar Shahib et al. v. Canada (Minister of Citizenship and Immigration)
, [2005] F.T.R. Uned. 747; 2005 FC 1250; [2005] F.C.J. No. 1509, at para. 20;
Bady-Badila v. Canada (Ministre de la Citoyenneté et de l'Immigration)
, [2003] F.T.R. Uned. 240; 2003 FCT 399; [2003] F.C.J. No. 559.
