In
Augier
, supra, the Citizenship Officer had determined that since the applicant was born out wedlock, outside of Canada, on May 9, 1966, pursuant to the legislation then in force, Canadian citizenship could only be derived from his mother. If the applicant's parents had been married at the time of his birth, then he could have derived Canadian citizenship from his father. However, since his natural parents were not married and the applicant's mother was not a Canadian citizen at time of the applicant's birth, the Citizenship Officer refused his application for proof of citizenship.
