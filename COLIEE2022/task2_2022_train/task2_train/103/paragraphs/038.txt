  The Supreme Court of Canada has held that the level of deference to be granted to an administrative decision maker depends on whether the role it is performing is one of balancing the interests between competing constituencies, or of establishing the rights and obligations of a party: see  
 Pushpanathan 
 , above at paragraph 36. A greater level of deference is to be afforded to the Board when it is engaged in the former role.
