The standard of proof to be applied in these cases consists in determining whether the Crown, on whom the burden rests, has proven that there are "serious reasons for considering" that the male applicant committed crimes against humanity:
Ramirez
, supra;
Moreno and Sanchez v. Minister of Employment and Immigration
, [1994] 1 F.C. 298; 159 N.R. 210 (F.C.A.);
Sivakumar v. Minister of Employment and Immigration
, [1994] 1 F.C. 433; 163 N.R. 197 (F.C.A.). This standard requires more than mere suspicion or conjecture but less than the civil standard of proof on a balance of probabilities:
Sivakumar
, supra.
