Thurlow, C.J., wrote that the tortious conversion levied against the defendants here must be that which is contemplated by the law of the Province of Newfoundland. He referred to the Crown Liability Act as the means whereby the liability of the Crown, for the alleged tort of the Canadian Saltfish Corporation, may arise. But the Chief Justice went on to opine that the claim pursued under s. 6(9) of the Coastal Fisheries Act was, in his opinion, not one in tort.
