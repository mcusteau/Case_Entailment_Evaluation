  In support of his argument that the Board erred in finding that the AFRC's purpose was limited and brutal, the applicant relies on  
 Balta v. Canada (Minister of Citizenship and Immigration) 
 , [1995] F.C.J. No. 146; 91 F.T.R. 81 (T.D.), at para. 14 (T.D.), wherein the Serbian Army, which had committed international crimes in the Balkans, was found by the Court not to be an organization with a limited and brutal purpose because it had a political objective, namely Serbian control of Bosnia.
