Second, the respondent pointed out that, for purposes of comparison, the first portion of a trademark should be treated as being of particular significance:
Molson Companies Ltd. v. Labatt (John) Ltd.
(1990), 32 F.T.R. 152; 28 C.P.R.(3d) 457 (T.D.), at p. 461 [C.P.R.]. Hence, the fact that "Greta" is the first part of the respondent's trademark goes a long way towards establishing that the marks are not similar in appearance or sound.
