  Second, as to what constitutes persecution, the leading case is  
 Rajudeen v. Minister of Employment and Immigration 
  (1984), 55 N.R. 129 (F.C.A.), where the Federal Court of Appeal at page 133 defined persecution in terms of to harass or afflict with repeated acts of cruelty or annoyance; to afflict persistently; to afflict or punish because of particular opinions or adherence to a particular creed or mode of worship; a particular course or period of systematic infliction of punishment directed against those holding a particular belief; persistent injury or annoyance from any source.
