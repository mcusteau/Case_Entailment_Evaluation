  From the outset it is important to determine just what the applicants' claim was comprised of. It is well established that in order to succeed in a claim to be a Convention refugee the claimant must show that the persecution which is feared is linked to one of the five Convention grounds (See:  
 Rizkallah v. Minister of Employment and Immigration 
  (1992), 156 N.R. 1 (F.C.A.)).
