  In the case at bar the only steps taken by the applicants to seek state protection was contacting the Gualan local police who in turn contacted the Guatemala City police. It is trite law that the refusal of local police to offer protection does not constitute inadequate state protection, unless the refusal of the local police can be linked to a larger pattern of inability to offer protection in the particular circumstances ( 
 Zhuravlvev v. Canada (MCI) 
 , [2000] 4 F.C. 3, per Justice Pelletier at para. 31).
