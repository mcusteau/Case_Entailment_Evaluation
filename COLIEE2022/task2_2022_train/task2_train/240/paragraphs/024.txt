Two elements are required to establish a claimant's "well-founded fear" of persecution. Fear must be felt subjectively by the claimant and this fear must be well founded in an objective sense: Rajudeen v. Canada (Minister of Employment and Immigration) (1984), 55 N.R. 129 (F.C.A.); Canada (Attorney General) v. Ward, [1993] 1 S.C.R. 689.
