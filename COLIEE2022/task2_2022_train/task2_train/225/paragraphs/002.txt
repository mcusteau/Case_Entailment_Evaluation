  The sole issue in this judicial review proceeding is whether the credibility findings made by the tribunal, being findings of fact, constituted a decision made within the terms of s. 18.1(4)(d) of the  
 Federal Courts Act, 1998 
  i.e. a decision made on a finding of fact arrived at in a perverse or capricious manner or without regard for the material before it, a standard equivalent to the standard of review of patent unreasonableness meaning clear irrationality, see  
 Ryan v. Law Society of New Brunswick
