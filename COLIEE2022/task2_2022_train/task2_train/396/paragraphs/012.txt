Counsel for the applicant agreed that s. 70(5) gives a discretionary power to the respondent, and that where a decision under the subsection could be shown to be a reasonable conclusion in regard to the totality of the evidence, the court should not intervene, even if it would have come to a different conclusion. However, he argued that the decision in
Nguyen
was limited by its context, which was a challenge to the constitutionality of the legislative provisions there under review, and that it was not a statement regarding the authority and jurisdiction of the court to review decisions made pursuant to the provisions, or as to the degree of deference to be accorded such opinions. Indeed, counsel for the applicant emphasized that the court in
Nguyen
confirmed the jurisdiction of the court to review such decisions.
