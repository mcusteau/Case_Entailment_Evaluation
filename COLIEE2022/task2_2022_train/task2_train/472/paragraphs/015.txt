Even though normally, delay is not a determinative factor in a refugee claim, (as mentioned in
Huerta v. Canada (Ministre de la Citoyenneté et de l'Immigration)
, [1993] F.C.J. No. 271; 157 N.R. 225 (F.C.A.), which was cited by the Board in its decision), it has been found that in certain circumstances, it could be so (
Gamassi v. Canada (Ministre de la Citoyenneté et de l'Immigration)
, [2000] F.C.J. No. 1841; 194 F.T.R. 178 (T.D.), at para. 6). I agree with the respondent that it is for the Board to decide, on the basis of the evidence before it, the significance of delay in a particular case (
Canada (Minister of Citizenship and Immigration) v. Sivalingam-Yogarajah
, [2001] F.T.R. Uned. 638; [2001] F.C.J. No. 1414; 2001FCT 1018 (T.D.), at para. 18).
