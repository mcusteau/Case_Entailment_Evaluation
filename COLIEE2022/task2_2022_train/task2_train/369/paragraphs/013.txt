In my opinion, the Citizenship Judge's focus on the number of days of absence prevented him from considering the wealth of evidence on the record from which he could have concluded that, despite the significant absences, given the Applicants' unique lifestyle of constantly being on the move for business reasons, and also the creation of their family and social life in Canada, the Applicants had no more substantial connection with any other country in the world than Canada. Performing the missing step in the analysis could have resulted in a completely different decision.
