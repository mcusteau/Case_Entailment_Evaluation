However, counsel for applicants point out that what they really seek is a judicial determination as to the locking in of dates of application for the purposes of determining points to be allotted for all applications prior to January 30, 1991, when occupational points for all occupations were fixed at zero. A concession such as that offered in the
Yeung
case merely applies to that case, and such a consent judgment does not necessarily establish the principle to be applied to all cases in this category, of which there are allegedly thousands now in the system. It is contended that it would be ridiculous to force each such applicant to make an independent motion to the court to obtain the same redress, and to make the same arguments. There is therefore a valid reason for refusing to accept Confessions of Judgment in three motions on terms that do not establish the principle to be applied to all such cases.
