He was interviewed in March 1990 and by letter dated April 9, 1990 was advised that he did not meet the requirements but as he had indicated that he had a relative in Canada willing to assist, the relative should contact the C.I.C. office in his area to complete an Undertaking of Assistance. This is what is known as an R-2 letter and at the time, it is contended, the Undertaking of Assistance form 1344 was only available upon presentation of such an R-2 letter. It was obtained by the relative, executed and submitted as of December 5, 1990 at which date the occupational demand for "manager, sales" was one point.
