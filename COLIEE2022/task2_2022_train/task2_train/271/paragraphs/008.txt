The applicable standard rule of review for a decision of the Board is whether it is patently unreasonable. As stated by Pelletier, J., in
Conkova et al. v. Canada (Minister of Citizenship and Immigration)
[see footnote 1]
"The standard of review of decisions of the CRDD is generally patent unreasonableness except for questions involving the interpretation of a statute when the standard becomes correctness.
Sivasamboo v. Canada
, [1995] 1 F.C. 741 (T.D.); (1994) 87 F.T.R. 46;
Pushpanathan v. Canada
, [1998] 1 S.C.R. 982; (1998) 160 D.L.R.(4th) 193."
