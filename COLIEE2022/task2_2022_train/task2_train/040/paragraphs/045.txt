  I was of the view that the existing jurisprudence had already determined, in a satisfactory manner, the degree of deference to be accorded to the RPD as to the definition of a refugee. No deference was owed. I referred to the recent decision of  
 Feimi v Canada (Minister of Citizenship and Immigration) 
 , 2012 FCA 325, [2012] FCJ No 1610 (QL), wherein Mr. Justice Evans, with the concurrence of Madam Justice Sharlow, applied the correctness standard. Mr. Justice Stratas was of the view that for the purposes of that case it was not necessary to rule on the standard of review.
