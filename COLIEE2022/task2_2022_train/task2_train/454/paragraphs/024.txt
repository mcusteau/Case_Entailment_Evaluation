The Respondent submits that it was open to the RAD to confirm the RPD's assessment of the Riback Report. The RPD had several credibility concerns, only one of which was related to paternity. Once a negative credibility finding has been made, it is open to the decision-maker to give low probative value to documents that reflect the applicant's statements (
Giron v Canada (Citizenship and Immigration)
, 2008 FC 1377 at paras 11-12). For the same reasons, it was also open to the RAD to assign little weight to the Choi Report which was based on self-reporting rather than independent assessments. Regardless, the RAD concluded that the Applicants had failed to show that they could not obtain the recommended psychological treatment in the proposed IFA locations.
