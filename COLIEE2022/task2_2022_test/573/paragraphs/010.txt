Counsel for the Minister argued that the occupational factor is intended to be merely a measure of occupational demand in Canada. No doubt that is so. But it also asks whether the
applicant
meets the employment requirements, and whether the
applicant
has performed a substantial number of the main duties for the intended occupation. This part of the regulation on its face requires a determination of facts relating to the
applicant
, as well as occupational demand in Canada.
