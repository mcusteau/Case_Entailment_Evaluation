I am not persuaded, therefore, that the Associate Senior Prothonotary exercised his discretion on any wrong principle of law. He evidently believed, after hearing full argument on the question, that the plaintiffs do not have the particulars necessary to allow them to know the case they have to meet, and to define the matters in issue in order to prepare their Affidavit of Documents and file an adequate Reply to the Statement of Defence and Defence to the counterclaim. There are no grounds which would justify my interference with that decision.
