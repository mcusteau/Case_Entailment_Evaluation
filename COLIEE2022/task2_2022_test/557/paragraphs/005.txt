Questions of credibility are within the jurisdiction of the tribunal as the trier of fact in respect of Convention refugee claims, and a reviewing court should be hesitant in interfering. However, the Court has a duty to interfere when findings of credibility are patently unreasonable, or when they are made based on erroneous findings of fact made in a perverse or capricious manner without regard for the material before it. For the following reasons, this Court finds that the intervention is warranted here.
