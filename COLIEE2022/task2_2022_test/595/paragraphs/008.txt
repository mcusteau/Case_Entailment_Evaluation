It is well-established that findings of credibility and plausibility lie particularly within the Board's jurisdiction and, therefore, the Court's intervention is merited only when such findings are completely unreasonable (Aguebor v. Minister of Employment and Immigration) (1993), 160 N.R. 315 at para. 4 (F.C.A.)). The applicable standard of review for such findings is, therefore, patent unreasonableness.
