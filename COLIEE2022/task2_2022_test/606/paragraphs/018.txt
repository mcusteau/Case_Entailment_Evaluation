Third, it is notable that s. 53(1) of the former Act did not refer to s. 19(1)(c.1)(ii), the provision that was in issue in Legault. The latter provision described a person who there were reasonable grounds to believe had committed a crime. It did not require that the person be convicted. In that context, understandably, the Federal Court of Appeal held that consideration of an outstanding charge and warrant was permissible.
