The applicant argues that the test under s. 97 should be more objective than that applied for Convention refugee status under s. 96. He argues that subsection 97(1) mandates an objective analysis of a claimant's situation, with due regard to country conditions. The applicant argues that, in the present case, the adverse credibility determination made by the Board is not sufficient to exclude the applicant from being a person in need of protection because the Board must go on to analyse country conditions in Tunisia to determine whether he would be subject to a danger of torture or a risk to life or to a risk of cruel and unusual treatment or punishment.
