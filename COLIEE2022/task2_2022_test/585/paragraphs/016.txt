The respondent argues that s. 18.1(3)(b) contemplates a "determination" by the tribunal and such would be precluded, in any meaningful sense, by specific directions. This contention can be answered, in my view, by reference to two factors: s. 18.1(3) does not state that the court may issue only "general" directions - there is no restriction on the specificity of the directions which may be issued; the decisions in
Orelien
,
Chaudri
and
Attakora
(supra) were all decided by reference to a provision which required reference back to the tribunal for "determination".
