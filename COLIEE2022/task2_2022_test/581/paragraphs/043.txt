The Minister has relied on a number of decisions where this Court held it was open to the Board to consider documentary evidence to the effect that fraudulent documents are “widespread”, “rampant” and “can easily be obtained” in Pakistan. But in all of these cases, the Board had already found other inconsistencies or credibility flaws in the claimant’s story. This is not the case here. Nor is it a case where the Board was able to look at other examples of similar documentation, and compare it to the documents submitted by the claimant. That was the situation in Uddin v. Canada (Minister of Citizenship and Immigration), 2002 FCT 451, on which the Minister relies.
