The only matter which could possibly be characterized as relating to bad faith is the alleged failure of Mr. Hayman to alert the applicant that it had already missed one deadline, and that the second was soon approaching. Mr. Hayman recalls that he had discussed the deadlines. However, giving VR Interactive the benefit of the doubt, it only says that Mr. Hayman was silent. There is no question of his making any sort of a representation which could possibly be considered as granting an extension of the delays. Everyone is presumed to know the law, and Mr. Hayman certainly had no duty to warn.
