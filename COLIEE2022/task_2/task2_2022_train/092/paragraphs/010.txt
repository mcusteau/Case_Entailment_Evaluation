  The applicant argues that the second issue is a question of law for which the applicable standard of review is correctness. The second issue is whether the Board erred in its assessment of whether the applicant is a person in need of protection under the Act. I am of the view that this raises a question of mixed fact and law for which the standard of review is reasonableness simpliciter: Jayesekara v. Canada (Minister of Citizenship and Immigration), 2001 FCT 1014, [2001] F.C.J. No. 1393 (QL).
