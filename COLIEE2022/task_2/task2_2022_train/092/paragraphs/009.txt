  The first issue raised by the applicant involves plausibility and credibility determinations which are findings of fact. The jurisprudence has held that such findings are usually afforded the highest level of deference on judicial review. The standard of review for findings of fact by an administrative tribunal is "patent unreasonableness": Pushpanathan v. Canada (Minister of Citizenship and Immigration), [1998] 1 S.C.R. 982, at para. 37; Aguebor v. Minister of Employment and Immigration (1993), 160 N.R. 315 (F.C.A.).
