In view of the nature of the arguments which the applicant could have used to challenge the respondent's decision, is the alternate remedy adequate in the case at bar or equivalent to a judicial remedy? Here the applicant disclosed in his own affidavit that he could object to the brief reasons given by the respondent, simply by denying them. However, could he by a grievance object in the intelligent and adequate manner described by Hugessen, J.A., in
Demaria
, supra, at p. 77?
"Where, as here, it is not intended to hold a hearing or otherwise give the person concerned a right to confront the evidence against him directly, it is particularly important that the notice contain as much detail as possible, else the right to answer becomes wholly illusory."
