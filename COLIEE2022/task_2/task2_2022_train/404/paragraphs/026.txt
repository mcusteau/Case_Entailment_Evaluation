If the court had found that there was a breach of natural justice it would have had doubts as to whether the grievance procedure was adequate in the circumstances. The procedure provides for no hearing at which the applicant could put forward new evidence such as Claude Véronneau's affidavit. The court notes the fact that the applicant only learned unofficially and in a clandestine way of Claude Véronneau and his involvement in a conspiracy to import a firearm at the same time that he was himself under suspicion.
