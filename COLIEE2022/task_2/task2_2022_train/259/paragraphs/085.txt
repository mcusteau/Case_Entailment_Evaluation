It has been established by the Supreme Court of Canada on several occasions such as
Biolyse
, supra, at paragraphs 470 and 473 and
Bell ExpressVu Limited Partnership v. Rex et al.
, [2002] 2 S.C.R. 559; 287 N.R. 248; 166 B.C.A.C. 1; 271 W.A.C. 1; 2002 SCC 42 at paragraphs 26 and 27 that the words of a statute or regulation are to be read in their entire context and in their grammatical and ordinary sense harmoniously with the scheme of the statute or regulation, the object of the statute or regulation, and the intention of Parliament.
