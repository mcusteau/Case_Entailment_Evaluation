  The moving party bears the legal onus of establishing the facts necessary to obtain summary judgment. However, when that onus is met, the responding party has the evidential burden of showing that there is a genuine issue for trial. Both parties must put their best foot forward to enable the motions judge to determine whether there is an issue that should go to trial. See:  
 Von Langsdorff (F.) Licensing Ltd. v. S.F. Concrete Technology Inc. 
  (1999), 165 F.T.R. 74 (T.D.), at paragraph 12.
