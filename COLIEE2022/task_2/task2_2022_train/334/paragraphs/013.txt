Gao inquired into the status of his application for landing several times in the months following the Glendale report. A March 1991 letter from Immigration officials in reply to his two February 1991 letters reads in part:
"We understand your eagerness to have your case resolved and can assure you that we are working as quickly as possible to bring it to a conclusion. Presently we are waiting for the results of your son's medical examination.
"We expect the results from Health and Welfare shortly and will then be able to advise you as to the status of your case.
. . . . .
"We hope to be contacting you soon with more information concerning your case."
