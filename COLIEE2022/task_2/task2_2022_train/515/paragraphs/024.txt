Equally, the above legislative scheme grants to the RPD a substantial amount of discretion to do what it deems to be required in order to enable a "full and proper hearing". In addition to the power to compel evidence by summoning witnesses or ordering the production of documents, the RPD "may do any other thing they consider necessary". The RPD is, in short, "master of its own procedure" (see for e.g.,
Sutton v. Canada Employment and Immigration Commission
(1994), 74 F.T.R. 284 (T.D.)).
