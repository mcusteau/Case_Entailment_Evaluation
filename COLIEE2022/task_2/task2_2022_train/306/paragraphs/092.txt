Very early in his argument counsel for the defendant emphasized,
"You will hear evidence that the only advantage that the patent says is given by this ... that of reducing the blind spot ... was already accomplished by the prior art, by the base rotation booms and by the axial rotation booms, also that that problem does not exist in the real world because the booms which are in issue here ... the sale to Denison ... were all two-boom machines, so that if one boom had a blind spot that it cannot reach, the other boom can reach it.
"There is prior art which will also be led. One case of prior art is the Madison Patent which will be used for both anticipation and obviousness."
