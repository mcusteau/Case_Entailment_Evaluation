  At the same time, Mr. Justice Muldoon in  
 Jimenez v. Canada (Minister of Citizenship and Immigration) 
  (1998), 147 F.T.R. 199, held that a decision by the relevant immigration officer that the applicant "appear[ed] to meet the eligibility requirements" of the DROC class rendered the decision-maker functus officio so that that decision could not be reopened to allow the immigration officer to consider evidence that the applicant may have committed war crimes or crimes against humanity.
