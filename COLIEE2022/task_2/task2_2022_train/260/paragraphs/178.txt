The soundness of a prediction is a question of fact and is to be assessed based upon information and expertise available at the relevant time (i.e.: date of the patent application) (
Aventis Pharma Inc. v. Apotex Inc. et al.
(2006), 349 N.R. 183; 265 D.L.R.(4th) 308; 2006 FCA 64, at para. 29; reference is also made to
Searle (G.D.) & Co. et al. v. Novopharm Ltd. et al.
(2007), 296 F.T.R. 254; 2007 FC 81, at paras. 98, 102, 103). To establish sound prediction, it is necessary to show: (a) a factual basis for the prediction, (b) an articulable and "sound" line of reasoning from which the desired result can be inferred from the factual basis, and (c) a proper disclosure. (
Apotex v. Wellcome Foundation
, above at para. 70.)
