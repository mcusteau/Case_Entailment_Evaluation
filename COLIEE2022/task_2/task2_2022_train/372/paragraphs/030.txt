The Board is in the best position to gauge the credibility and plausibility of the Applicant and to draw the necessary inferences. As long as those inferences are not so unreasonable as to warrant the Court's intervention, the Board's findings are not open to judicial review (Aguebor v. Canada (Minister of Employment and Immigration) (1993), 160 N.R. 315 (F.C.A.); Araya v. Canada (Minister of Citizenship and Immigration), [2003] F.C.J. 821(T.D.)).
