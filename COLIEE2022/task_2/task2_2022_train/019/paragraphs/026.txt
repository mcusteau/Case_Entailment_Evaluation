  Both parties acknowledge that pursuant to Rule 22 of the  
 Federal Court Immigration and Refugee Protection Rules 
 , SOR/2002-232, special reasons must exist for the Court to award costs on application for judicial review. Special reasons may be found if one party has unnecessarily or unreasonably prolonged proceedings, or where one party has acted in a manner that may be characterized as unfair, oppressive, improper or actuated by bad faith.
