Similarly, in the present case, the payments made to the transferring air traffic controllers did not arise by virtue of the contract of employment but rather pursuant to a separate agreement, which was entered into before the effective date of the transfers as opposed to after termination of the employment as in Roy (supra). But more importantly, the payment of the allowance with which I am concerned was primarily motivated by considerations extraneous to the employment, namely public and labour relations considerations. This is further evidenced by the requirement that the transferring employees avoid contact with the media and not reside in the Province of Quebec for five years, and, further, failing which they would have to reimburse.
