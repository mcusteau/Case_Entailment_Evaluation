Non-citizens do not have an unqualified right to enter or remain in Canada. In
Chiarelli v. Minister of Employment and Immigration
, [1992] 1 S.C.R. 711; 135 N.R. 161; 1992 CanLII 87, at page 733, Justice John Sopinka stated that "[t]he most fundamental principle of immigration law is that non-citizens do not have an unqualified right to enter or remain in the country".
