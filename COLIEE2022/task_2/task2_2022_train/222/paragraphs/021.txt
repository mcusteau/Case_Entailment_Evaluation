  These decisions draw on the principles respecting the adequacy of reasons outlined by Justice Sexton for the Federal Court of Appeal in Via Rail Canada Inc. v. National Transportation Agency, [2001] 2 F.C. 25, 193 D.L.R. (4th) 357 [Via Rail] and cited with approval by this Court on numerous occasions: Abdollahi-Ghane v. Canada (Attorney General) (2004), 259 F.T.R. 9, 2004 FC 741; Alexander v. Canada (Solicitor General) (2005), 49 Imm. L.R. (3d) 5, 2005 FC 1147; Demirovic v. Canada (Minister of Citizenship and Immigration), 2005 FC 1284, [2005] F.C.J. No. 1560 (QL).
