The court has to be aware, however, that interrogatories can pose something of a problem for the party drafting the questions: there is no opportunity to clarify a question which is deliberately or even honestly misunderstood; it may be difficult to foresee an unclear or evasive answer; it may sometimes be necessary to put a large number of questions of a "follow-up" nature based upon supposition or hypothesis as to what the answer to an earlier question will be.
