  The Supreme Court of Canada has held that there are only two standards of review: correctness for questions of law and reasonableness involving questions of mixed fact and law and fact:  
 Dunsmuir v New Brunswick 
 , 2008 SCC 9, [2008] 1 SCR 190 [Dunsmuir] at paras 50 and 53. The Supreme Court has also held that where the standard of review has been previously determined, a standard of review analysis need not be repeated:  
 Dunsmuir 
  at para 62.
