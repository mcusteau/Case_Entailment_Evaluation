The Applicant focuses on two issues: first, she argues that the decision not to deal with her complaint involves a determination whether the Commission properly acted within its jurisdiction. The Applicant refers to and relies on section 41(1) of the
Act
which requires the Commission to deal "with any complaint filed with it", subject to the exceptions provided in the
Act
. The Applicant argues that the decision not to deal with her complaint necessarily leads to an inquiry of whether the Commission erroneously failed to exercise its jurisdiction.
