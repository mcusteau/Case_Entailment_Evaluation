  First, the respondent submits that the Board did not base its decision on any erroneous findings of fact which were perverse, capricious or made without regard to the material before it. The respondent argues that the Board is authorized to assess the relevance of evidence, determine the weight to be attached to it and to draw inferences therefrom. Further, the fact that the written reasons did not summarize all of the facts does not constitute a reviewable error. It is enough that the reasons of the Board demonstrate a grasp of the pertinent issues and the relevance of the evidence. There is no obligation to list every conceivable factor that might have influenced its decision.
