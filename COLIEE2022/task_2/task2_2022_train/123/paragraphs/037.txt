  First, the Board considered it significant that there was no State complicity in the local police force's actions. It is now trite law that this is not determinative. The question that must have been asked and was not asked was whether the State was able to or willing to protect the applicant. Although the applicant has the burden of proving that the State could not or would not protect her she cannot do this when the issue was not considered.
