In
Benner
, the Supreme Court concluded that the legislation in question failed on the first branch of the second requirement - rational connection - and therefore was not justified under section 1 of the
Charter
. More particularly, the Supreme Court declared that paragraph 3(1)(e), paragraph 5(2)(b), and section 22 of the current
Citizenship Act
, R.S.C., c. C-29, as well as section 20 of the
Citizenship Regulations
, C.R.C., c. 400, did not constitute a reasonable limit prescribed by law pursuant to section 1 of the
Charter
.
