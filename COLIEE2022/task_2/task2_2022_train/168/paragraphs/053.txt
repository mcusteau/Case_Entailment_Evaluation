  In the criminal context, the Supreme Court has found that cruel or unusual treatment or punishment is, among other things, treatment that is so excessive that it outrages standards of decency, treatment that is grossly disproportionate to the offence, shocks the conscience of Canadians, or is simply unacceptable:  
 R. v. Smith (E.D.) 
 , [1987] 1 S.C.R. 1045; 75 N.R. 321; Peter W. Hogg,  
 Constitutional Law of Canada 
  (Carswell Canada), at p. 50-53;  
 Schmidt v. Canada et al. 
 , [1987] 1 S.C.R. 500; 76 N.R. 12; 20 O.A.C. 161;  
 United States of America v. Allard and Charette 
 , [1987] 1 S.C.R. 564; 75 N.R. 260; 8 Q.A.C. 178.
