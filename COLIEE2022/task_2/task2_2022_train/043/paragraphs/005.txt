  The applicant's hearing continued to worsen after his discharge and in 1991, he was diagnosed with bilateral moderately severe high frequency sensorineural hearing loss. The applicant subsequently applied for a hearing loss disability pension on the basis of his 1991 diagnosis. His application was denied on June 7, 1993 by the Canadian Pension Commission because the applicant's hearing loss at the time of his discharge was not sufficiently severe to be considered a disability as described in a report of the Pensions Medical Advisory Division dated May 20, 1993.
