The principles enunciated in
Rizzo & Rizzo Shoes Ltd. (Re)
, supra, were recently applied in
Francis v. Baker
, [1999] S.C.J. No. 52; 246 N.R. 45; 125 O.A.C. 201 (S.C.C.), a case concerning the interpretation of a provision of the
Federal Child Support Guidelines
. In that decision, Bastarache, J., writing for the court, noted in paragraph 35 that "[p]roper statutory interpretation principles ... require that all evidence of legislative intent must be considered, provided that it is relevant and reliable".
