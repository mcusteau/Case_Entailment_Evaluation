From this reasoning, therefore, it appears that the applicant in this case may have been charged with the wrong offence. The officers who were present when the applicant stated that if he were put into General Population he would kill the next officer he saw were clearly not the subject of the threat. Therefore, it may be that the applicant did not behave towards them in a threatening manner. What the applicant did in fact do was threaten to assault another person; that is, as I perceive it, the offence contemplated by paragraph 39(b).
