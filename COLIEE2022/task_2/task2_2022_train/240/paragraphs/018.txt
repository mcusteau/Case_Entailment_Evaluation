The applicant submits that relevant evidence before the Board was not considered in determining her claim. The documentary evidence on country conditions contained numerous references which support the claim and the Board's failure to mention this evidence in its reasons constitutes reviewable error: Padilla v. Canada (Minister of Employment and Immigration) (1991), 13 Imm. L.R. (2d) 1; Toro v. Canada (Minister of Employment and Immigration), [1981] 1 F.C. 656 (F.C.A.); Tung v. Canada (Minister of Employment and Immigration) (1991), 124 N.R. 388 (F.C.A.).
