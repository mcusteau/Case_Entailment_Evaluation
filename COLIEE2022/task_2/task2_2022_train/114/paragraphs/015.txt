  Essentially, the petitioner argues that there always remains a continuum of rights and freedoms guaranteed and protected in the cycle of defendant-prisoner-parolee-free citizen, which rights and freedoms are constricted or enlarged in proportion to the individual's status at any given moment during that cycle: thus, even as a parolee, he retains the residue of such rights as are protected by the Charter. He is still free to enjoy all the civil rights of a person, save those that are taken away.
