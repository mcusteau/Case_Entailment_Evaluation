The applicant submits that he acted swiftly in requesting the documents from the Court office, in picking up the documents as soon as they were ready and that therefore, it is reasonable to assume that he acted swiftly in delivering them to Immigration Canada. He argues that the respondent's representative had no personal knowledge of when the documents were delivered, and that she was relying solely on the date stamp and her knowledge of departmental procedures. He further argues that she stated in cross-examination that it was possible that the documents could have been delivered on January 27, 1993.
