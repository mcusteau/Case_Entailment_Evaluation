Rule 400(1) of the
Federal Courts Rules
gives the Federal Court the discretionary power to award solicitor-client costs. However, the awarding of solicitor-client costs should only be made where a party has displayed reprehensible, scandalous or outrageous conduct (
Young v. Young
, [1993] 4 S.C.R. 3 at para. 251;
Mackin v. New Brunswick (Minister of Finance); Rice v. New Brunswick
, [2002] 1 S.C.R. 405 at para. 86;
Apotex Inc. v. Canada (Minister of National Health and Welfare)
(2000), 9 C.P.R. (4th) 289 at para. 7 (F.C.A.)). Reasons of public interest may also justify making an award of solicitor-client costs (
Friends of the Oldman River Society v. Canada (Minister of Transport)
, [1992] 1 S.C.R. 3 at 80).
