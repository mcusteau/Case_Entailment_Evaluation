  The Board found that in this case, the length of Mr. Romans's detention was of particular importance. While Mr. Romans had been detained for more than five years, his case was far from resolved. There were at least three processes outstanding, and the Minister had given an undertaking not to remove Mr. Romans until such time as the UNHRC dealt with the case. In these circumstances, the Board found that there was simply no way of knowing when, if at all, Mr. Romans would be removed from Canada.
