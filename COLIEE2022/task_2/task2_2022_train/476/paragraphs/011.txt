The respondent submits that the Board's findings with respect credibility and implausibility are findings of fact reviewable on the standard of unreasonableness (
Dunsmuir v. New Brunswick
, 2008 SCC 9, [2008] S.C.J. No. 9). The decision maker's credibility analysis is central to its role as trier of fact, and consequently its Credibility findings are entitled to the highest degree of curial deference and therefore set a heavy onus on the applicant seeking to set aside such a finding.
