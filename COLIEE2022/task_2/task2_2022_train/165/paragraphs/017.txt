  Despite not being satisfied with his identity demonstration, the RPD continued with its analysis, concluding that even had the applicant sufficiently demonstrated his identity, his refugee claim would still have been denied because it lacked credibility. Notably, the RPD drew negative inferences from the fact that the applicant had amended his original Basis of Claim form [BOC] to reflect that his father's stroke occurred between his two arrests (and not following his second arrest). The Board considered this to be a significant event in the applicant's life, and so he should not have made a chronological error in this respect.
