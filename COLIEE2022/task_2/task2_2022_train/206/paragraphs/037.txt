  The obligation of a tribunal was succinctly described by the Ontario Court of Appeal in  
 Clifford v. Ontario Municipal Employees Retirement System 
 , 2009 ONCA 670, [2009] O.J. No. 3900 (QL) at paragraph 40: 
 
 
 
 . . . the majority faulted the Tribunal for not referring to evidence that could have led it to decide differently. Again, I disagree. As I have described, reasons need not refer to every piece of evidence to be sufficient, but  
 must simply provide an adequate explanation of the basis upon which the decision was reached 
 . [Emphasis added.]
