Counsel for the judgment debtor has presented two decisions dealing with the issue of jurisdiction of the Federal Court as it affects this application. In Union Oil Company of Canada Limited v. The Queen in right of Canada and the Queen in right of the Province of British Columbia (1974), 52 D.L.R.(3d) 388 (F.C.T.D.); [1976] 1 F.C. 74 (F.C.A.); (1976), 16 N.R. 425 (S.C.C.), a decision upheld by the Federal Court of Appeal and the Supreme Court of Canada, my colleague Collier, J., granted a motion to strike out the Provincial Crown as a defendant. He stated that "the Crown" referred to in s. 17(1) is by definition Her Majesty in right of Canada.
