In
Augusto v. Canada (Solicitor General)
, 2005 FC 673, [2005] F.C.J. No. 850 (QL), at para. 9 my colleague Justice Layden-Stevenson held that "[i] n the absence of having failed to consider relevant factors or having relied upon irrelevant ones, the weighing of the evidence lies within the purview of the [PRRA] officer conducting the assessment and does not normally give rise to judicial review."
