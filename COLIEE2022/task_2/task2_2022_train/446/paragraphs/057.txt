As a general rule, the courts should endeavour to avoid expressing an opinion on a question of law where it is not necessary to do so in order to dispose of a case, especially when the question of law that need not be decided is a constitutional question:
Glassco and Quebec (Attorney General) v. Cumming
, [1978] 2 S.C.R. 605; 22 N.R. 271, at 611 [S.C.R.];
Phillips et al. v. Richard, J.
, [1995] 2 S.C.R. 97; 180 N.R. 1; 141 N.S.R.(2d) 1; 403 A.P.R. 1; 124 D.L.R.(4th) 129, at para 9;
Daigle v. Tremblay
, [1989] 2 S.C.R. 530; 102 N.R. 81; 27 Q.A.C. 81; 62 D.L.R.(4th) 634.
