The Respondent submits that the RPD did not commit an error by considering the Applicant's delay in claiming refugee status. While delay in submitting a claim cannot be the reason for its dismissal, the Federal Court in
Singh v. Canada (Minister of Citizenship and Immigration)
, [2003] F.C.J. No. 1451 (F.C.) at paragraph 29 has held that it is a factor to consider:
Delay in applying for Convention refugee status is not an automatic bar to a claim for protection. However, it is a relevant and potentially important consideration. Ultimately, the Board must decide, based on the evidence before it, the significance of a delay to a particular case.
