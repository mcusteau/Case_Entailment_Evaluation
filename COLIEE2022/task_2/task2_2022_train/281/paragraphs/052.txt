Section 56 of the
Act
specifically provides for a right to appeal a decision of the Board to the Federal Court. Moreover, s. 56(5) provides that on appeal the parties may adduce additional evidence that was not before the Board. However, the mere existence of a statutory right of appeal is not sufficient to dictate the standard by which the Court will review the Board's decision. As reaffirmed by the Supreme Court of Canada in
Dr. Q., Re
, [2003] 1 S.C.R. 226; 302 N.R. 34; 179 B.C.A.C. 170; 295 W.A.C. 170, the Court must still determine the appropriate standard of review by conducting a pragmatic and functional analysis.
