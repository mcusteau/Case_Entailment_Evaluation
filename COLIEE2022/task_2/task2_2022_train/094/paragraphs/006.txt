  It is well settled that judicial review of an administrative decision is made on the basis of the evidence that was before the decision-maker. Additional evidence is only admissible in very limited circumstances, where it may be needed to resolve issues of procedural fairness or jurisdiction: Ontario Association of Architects v. Association of Architectural Technologists of Ontario, [2003] 1 F.C. 331 at para. 30, 2002 FCA 218; Covarrubias v. Canada (Minister of Citizenship and Immigration) (2005), 48 Imm. L.R. (3d) 186 at para. 13, 17-19, 2005 FC 1193; Asafov v. Canada (Minister of Employment and Immigration), [1994] F.C.J. No. 713 at paras. 1-2 (F.C.T.D.) (QL).
