The learned author Driedger suggests that if a Minister's speech be inadmissible to show intent, then, logically it might be "more" admissible to show "the evil or mischief" (which provoked introduction of the Bill). However, the learned author notes (at pp. 130-131), "Perhaps the reason for rejecting a Minister's speech in Parliament to show mischief is that a Royal Commission is supposed to be objective, and evidence tendered to it is normally given under oath."
