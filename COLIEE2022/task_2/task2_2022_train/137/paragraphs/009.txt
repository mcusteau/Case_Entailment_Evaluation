  A stay of proceedings for an abuse of process is an extraordinary remedy reserved for the clearest cases of prejudice. To grant that remedy, "the court must be satisfied that, 'the damage to the public interest in the fairness of the administrative process should the proceeding go ahead would exceed the harm to the public interest in the enforcement of the legislation if the proceedings were halted'" ( 
 Blencoe 
  at para 120, citing Brown and Evans,  
 Judicial Review of Administrative Action in Canada 
  (Toronto: Canvasback, 1998) at 9-68).
