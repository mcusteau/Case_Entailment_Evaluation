Had the Applicant been able to establish a risk to her life or a risk of cruel and unusual treatment or punishment, she would also have to show that this risk exists in the part of Sri Lanka specified in the IFA - Colombo. Again, it is well established that an individual need not pursue an Internal Flight Alternative if it is not objectively reasonable to do so or if it would cause undue hardship. In other words, and to quote Linden JA, "an IFA cannot be speculative or theoretical only; it must be a realistic, attainable option" (Thirunavukkarasu v. Canada (Minister of Employment and Immigration), [1994] 1 F.C. 589 (F.C.A.), at paragraph 14).
