First of all, it is difficult to believe, as the plaintiff would have it, that the money she was carrying was her "savings" and that she takes it with her whenever she leaves her house. Why, if that is the case, would she have precisely $10,000.00 in her money belt and $2,500.00 (all in $50.00 notes) in her purse? Why would a large proportion of her "savings" be in American dollars? Why would she split her "savings" in three different locations, that is, in her purse, in a money belt on her abdomen and in an envelope on her chest? That story, in and of itself, stretches the imagination.
