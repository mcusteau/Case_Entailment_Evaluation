In its Statement of Claim, the plaintiff alleges that the defendants infringed its exclusive rights contrary to the provisions of the
Copyright Act
, R.S.C. 1985, c. C-42, and have unlawfully converted the copyright works to its own use and benefit, contrary to the provisions of s. 38 of the
Copyright Act
. The plaintiff produced, as Schedules "A" through "H" to the Statement of Claim, copies of the certificates of registration of copyright in Canada for the artistic and literary works in issue. These certificates of registration of copyrights, dated January 1995, state that the plaintiff is the owner of one copyright in each of the works. These certificates also set out the authors of the works in question.
