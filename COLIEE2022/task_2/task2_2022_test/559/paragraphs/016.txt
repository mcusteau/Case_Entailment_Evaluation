In
New Brunswick (Board of Management) v. Dunsmuir
, [2008] 1 S.C.R. 190; 372 N.R. 1; 329 N.B.R.(2d) 1; 844 A.P.R. 1; [2008] S.C.J. No. 9 the Supreme Court of Canada stated that the process in determining the standard of review to apply involves first establishing whether the standard of review has already been established in jurisprudence involving similar circumstances. If a standard of review has already been cited then that standard would apply.
