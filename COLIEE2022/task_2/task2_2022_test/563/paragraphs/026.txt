In order for an alleged error of fact to be reviewable, it must be truly erroneous; the finding must be made capriciously or without regard to the evidence, and the decision must be based on the erroneous finding (Rohm and Haas Canada Ltd. v. Anti-Dumping Tribunal (1978), 22 N.R. 175, 91 D.L.R. (3d) 212 (F.C.A.); Federal Courts Act, R.S., 1985, c. F-7, s. 18.1; Bhuiyan et al. v. Canada (Minister of Employment and Immigration) (1993), 66 F.T.R. 310).
