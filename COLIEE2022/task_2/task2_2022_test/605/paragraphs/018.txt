The problem is precisely the one envisaged in Molnar, supra and underlying LaForest J.'s comments in Ward, supra: the police (the OJI) were the perpetrators of the applicant's persecution. Thus notwithstanding that not every member of the OJI was implicated in the applicant's persecution, seeking help from the OJI - asking, in effect, the OJI to protect the applicant from itself - would have in all likelihood placed the applicant in greater peril.
