The "Colorado" laid the foundation for the logic pursued in The "Strandhill", and subsequently, The "Har Rai", and The "Ioannis Daskelelis". In each of these cases, it was held that contracts for necessaries entered into in the United States will be treated before Canadian courts according to the laws of the United States with respect to the substance of the claims asserted, but ranked according to the Canadian law with respect to the priority of this type of claim in a distribution.
