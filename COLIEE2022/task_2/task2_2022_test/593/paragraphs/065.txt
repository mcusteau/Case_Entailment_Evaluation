In the first place, there is ample authority in this country which gives this court jurisdiction to recognize a maritime lien where a domestic trader would not be entitled to the benefit of one. Furthermore, as the Canadian court determines the nature of the claim according to the lex loci, even if the claim is not enumerated in the subsections of s. 22(2), (as in The "Har Rai"), the very fact that it is validly constituted as a maritime lien in the lex loci is sufficient to put it within the jurisdiction of the Federal Court.
