The
Thomson
case, of course, dealt with tax matters. I am not convinced that the quality of residence required to fulfil the requirements of the
Citizenship Act
admits of a similar interpretation. In my view to allow physical absence to be treated as residence within the country for the purposes of obtaining citizenship, the quality of the person's connection with this country must demonstrate a primacy or priority of residence in Canada (a more substantial connection with Canada than with any other place).
