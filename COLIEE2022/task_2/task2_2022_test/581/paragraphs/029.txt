In order to fairly use this information, the Board must provide adequate notice to claimants. Much like Rule 18, s. 68(5) of the former Immigration Act articulated that requirement. In Hassan, above, the Federal Court of Appeal concluded that by making the published information on country conditions publicly available and by referring to the then current Country Index at the outset of the hearing, the Board had adequately complied with the notice requirements of s. 68(5).
