  The main thrust of the applicants' argument is that the Board ignored or down-played evidence which was before it. The applicants submit further that the Board was obliged to consider and refer to the entirety of the applicants' evidence including the suspicious death of the man who purchased the applicant's car. This evidence was a crucial part of the circumstantial evidence that the applicant relied upon to show that the local police (1) were carrying out their threats against the applicants, (2) were responsible for attacks upon the applicants, (3) constituted a threat to the applicants' lives and (4) were actively pursuing the applicants.
