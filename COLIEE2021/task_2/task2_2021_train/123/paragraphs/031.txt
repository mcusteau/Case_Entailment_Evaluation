  First, the applicant claimed to have a well-founded fear of persecution based upon her membership in a particular group. That is, she claimed to have suffered persecution in the past and to be in danger from it in the future because of her link to her husband. Further, she claimed her son was attacked and is in future danger for the same reason. It would seem that immediate family can be seen to fit within the definition of "particular social group" (See  
 Astudillo v. Minister of Employment and Immigration 
  (1979), 31 N.R. 121 (F.C.A.);  
 Djama v. Canada (M.E.I.) 
  (June 15, 1992) Action No. A-783-90 (F.C.A.), and  
 Taheri v. Canada (M.E.I.) 
  (April 26, 1993) Action No. A-111-90 (F.C.A.)).
