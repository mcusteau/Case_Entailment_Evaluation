  Second, and more important, the Board erred in respect of the test to be applied in respect of a "Convention refugee" finding. The Board wrongly relied on its findings that there had been no State complicity or past persecution in coming to the conclusion that the applicants had not established a well-founded fear of persecution should they be returned to Bulgaria. The Board should have considered (1) whether there was any possibility of future persecution and (2) whether the State was unwilling or unable to protect the applicants.
