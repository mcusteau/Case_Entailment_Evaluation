On December 16, 2004, the Registrar of Trademarks, sitting as the Board, rejected the Applicant's opposition. The Board held that the Respondent had proven, on a balance of probabilities, that there is no reasonable likelihood of confusion between the Applicant's trademarks and the Respondent's proposed trademark. Moreover, the Board held that the Applicant had not established that the Respondent's trademark is not distinctive.
