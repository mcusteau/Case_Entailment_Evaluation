Second, the Board rejected the Applicant's submission that the existence of other trademarks in the Register with the word "tradition" is not relevant to whether or not the average consumer would likely be confused between the Respondent's and Applicant's trademarks, as there is no evidence that any of those other trademarks are used in association with baked goods. The Board disagreed, stating that the average consumer would be able to distinguish the origin of a muffin bearing the trademark TRADITION from a bagel sold in association with the Respondent's proposed trademark.
