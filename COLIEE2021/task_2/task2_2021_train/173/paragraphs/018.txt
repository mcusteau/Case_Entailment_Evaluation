  The RPD rejected their reasons for failing to regularize their status, noting that they are well-educated, claimed to fear serious persecution, and had plenty of time to become familiar with their various options. After noting that Ms. Velez and Mr. Gutierrez lived in an immigration neighbourhood in the United States, the RPD observed that many Colombian asylum claimants were being accepted in that country at that time and that many failed claimants had come to Canada and claimed refugee status here. Considering all of the evidence, together with the length of time without status in the United States and the seriousness of the harm feared, the RPD concluded that the principal Applicants' actions in the United States were not consistent with their stated fears.
