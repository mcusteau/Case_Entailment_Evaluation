  In any event, the visa officer was in no position to reconsider his decision concerning the Applicant even if additional information had been provided since the officer was functus officio after reaching his initial decision. The Respondent does not deny that visa officers have discretion to reconsider their decisions in certain limited circumstances where there has been a breach of natural justice or obvious errors or omissions such as an incorrect tally or addition of points, but this is not the situation here.
