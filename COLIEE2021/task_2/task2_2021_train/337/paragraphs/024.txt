By analogy, in the present case, the officer did not err by refusing Mr. Johnson's application for permanent residence. The convictions were in force when the negative decision was made and they remained in force until set aside on appeal. Again, I note that the consequence of upholding a deportation order on the basis that the conviction giving rise to it was valid at the time that the order was issued is of much more imminent effect than upholding, on the same basis, a negative decision on an inland application for permanent residence.
