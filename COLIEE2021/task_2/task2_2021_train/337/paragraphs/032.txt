First, while Mr. Johnson argues that the officer erred by failing to advise him that information had been received in respect of his criminal convictions, it was Mr. Johnson who had signed a form in which he agreed to notify CIC of any changes to the information he had provided to it, including changes to his answer that he had not been charged or convicted of a crime in Canada. His failure to meet that obligation should not shift the onus to the officer to communicate with Mr. Johnson about his convictions.
