  "Particular circumstances" are not just limited to the age and prior experience of an applicant. The court has also decided that the location, the treatment during detention and the manner of release are all equally relevant. As demands for bribes by the police are a form of extortion, they may also, in relevant circumstances, amount to "persecution" for the purposes of the Convention: see  
 Kularatnam 
   
 v. 
   
 Canada (Minister of Citizenship and Immigration) 
 , 2004 FC 1122, at paras. 10-13.
