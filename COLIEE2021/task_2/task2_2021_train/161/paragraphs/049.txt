  The assessment of risks upon return must be made on a case-by-case basis. Mr. Toora, considered not credible by the Board, has not related his personal situation to the general documentary evidence about the situation in the country: 
 
 
 
 "However, as MacGuigan, J.A., acknowledged in  
 Sheikh 
 , supra, in fact the claimant's oral testimony will often be the only evidence linking the claimant to the alleged persecution and, in such cases, if the claimant is not found to be credible, there will be no credible or trustworthy evidence to support the claim. Because they are not claimant-specific, country reports alone are normally not a sufficient basis on which the Board can uphold a claim.
