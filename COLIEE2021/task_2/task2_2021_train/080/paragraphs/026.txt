  On the issue of importance, it is the legal significance of the case, not the economic and business significance to the parties that must be considered ( 
 TRW 
 , supra, at p. 457). In  
 Balfour v. Norway House Cree Nation 
 , [2006] FC 616, [2006] F.C.J. No. 870 (QL), at para. 15, citing  
 Aird v. Country Party Village Property (Mainland) Ltd. 
 , [2004] FC 945, [2004] F.C.J. No. 1153 (QL), at para. 6, the Court emphasized "[r] egarding the importance and complexity of the issues, it is the legal significance and complexity, including the number of issues, that are to be considered and not the factual subject matter." (emphasis added).
