In
Jin
, the Court was urged by the respondent to follow
Zaki
and not
Thamotharem
. The Court declined to do so, noting that the two decisions were not in conflict on the central question of whether Guideline 7, in itself, breaches procedural fairness. The Court concluded that
Zaki
was distinguishable for the reasons cited by Justice Blanchard. The Court indicated it was fully in agreement with Justice Blanchard's analysis of the fettering issue.
