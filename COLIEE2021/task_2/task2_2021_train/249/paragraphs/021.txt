In
Ahir v. Minister of Employment and Immigration
, [1984] 1 F.C. 1098; 2 D.L.R.(4th) 163; 49 N.R. 185 (F.C.A.) (hereinafter, "
Ahir
") the Court of Appeal determined that an adjudicator, when conducting an inquiry in order to determine if a person is a member of an inadmissible class, is required to consider the reasonableness of the expectation by the medical officers that the person will cause excessive demands on health or social services.
