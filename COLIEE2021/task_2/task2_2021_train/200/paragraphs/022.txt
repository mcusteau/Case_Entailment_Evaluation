  The question whether an applicant has the relevant experience, training or education and requisite certificates, as required by the  
 Regulations 
  and thus qualified for the trade or profession in which he or she claims to be a skilled worker is "...based directly on the requirements of the legislation and regulations." and falls squarely within the reasoning of Mosley J. in  
 Hassani 
 . Therefore it was up to the applicant to submit sufficient evidence on this question and the Visa Officer was not under a duty to apprise him of his concerns or to conduct more detailed inquiries to resolve the latent ambiguity:  
 Kaur 
 , paras 9-12. Visa officers are not expected to engage in a dialogue with the applicant on whether the  
 Regulations 
  are satisfied.
