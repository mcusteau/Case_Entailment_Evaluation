Rouleau, J., put it this way in Philip Morris Inc. v. Imperial Tobacco Ltd., supra, at p. 275:
"... A 'customer' can be a wholesaler as well as a retailer, as long as the sale is made in the normal course of trade as defined by s. 4 of the Act. It has also been established that the words 'normal course of trade' recognize the continuity of a transaction from the manufacturer to the ultimate consumer and provide protection for the manufacturer's trademark throughout these intervening transactions ..."
