On February 23, 1988, at the offices of the Canadian Consulate General in New York, the applicant was interviewed by a visa officer regarding his application for permanent residence. The applicant states that during the interview he was not asked any questions dealing with his international reputation or contributions he believed he could make to the cultural or economic life in Canada resulting from his self-employment. Further, the applicant maintains he was never asked if he had self-employed experience in music, and the officer simply asked him whether he had been employed full-time in music. The applicant explained that he had been a full-time student and throughout had been teaching part-time and playing violin and viola.
