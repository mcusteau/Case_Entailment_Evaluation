Anticipation, therefore, has two requirements, disclosure and enablement. The assessment of enablement allows the skilled addressee to use common general knowledge and routine experimentation, including "trial and error experiments to get it to work". "The person skilled in the art can correct obvious errors and is allowed to use common general knowledge and routine skills to achieve the invention".
