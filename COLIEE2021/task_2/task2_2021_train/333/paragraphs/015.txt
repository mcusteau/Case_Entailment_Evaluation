The use of a trademark is defined in section 2 of the Trade Marks Act as meaning any use that by section 4 of the Act is deemed to be a use in association with wares or services. Dealing first with the appellant's contention based on section 4(3) of the Act, that provision states that a trademark is deemed to be used in Canada in association with wares if:
(a) the trademark is marked in Canada;
(b) on the wares or on the packages in which they are contained;
(c) when such wares are exported from Canada.
