  When Mr. Kim committed his criminal offences, he became inadmissible under the old  
 Immigration Act 
  and was ordered deported. Different categories of individuals are treated variously under the  
 Immigration Act 
  scheme, and the respondent argues, this is acceptable:  
 Chiarelli v. Minister of Employment and Immigration 
 , [1992] 1 S.C.R. 711; 135 N.R. 161. Permanent residents have no explicit statutory protection against removal to a state where they believe their life or freedom would be threatened:  
 Chieu v. Canada (Minister of Citizenship and Immigration) 
 , [2002] 1 S.C.R. 84; 280 N.R. 268, at 116-117 [S.C.R.];  
 Suresh v. Canada (Minister of Citizenship and Immigration) 
 , [2002] 1 S.C.R. 3.
