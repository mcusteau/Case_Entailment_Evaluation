  As I understand counsel for the applicant's argument, it is that in any event no assessment was ever made as to whether the husband, despite the fact that the marriage might have been entered into for the purpose of gaining admission to Canada, intended to reside permanently with the applicant. The two pronged test set out in  
 Horbas v. Minister of Employment and Immigration 
  (1986), 22 D.L.R.(4th) 600 (F.C.T.D.) was quoted. It is argued that it was not open to the Appeal Division to make determinations respecting the husband's intentions in this regard on the basis of a finding that the wife lacked credibility.
