  Finally, contrary to what was alleged by the applicant, the contradictions noted by the panel do not in any way appear to be based on errors. On the contrary, they clearly result from the grey areas and variations between the applicant's statements in his PIF, the statements made to the immigration officer and his testimony at the hearing. In the Court's view, the evidence justified the panel concluding that the applicant lacked credibility and finding that he was neither a "Convention refugee" nor a "person in need of protection" within the meaning of the Act.
