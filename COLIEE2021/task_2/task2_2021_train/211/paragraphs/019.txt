  Although no live controversy exists between the parties, the Court can still exercise its discretion to hear the case in the appropriate circumstances. Justice Sopinka, in  
 Borowski 
 , supra, identified three rationales that a reviewing court should consider in exercising such a discretion. Specifically, the Court should examine whether an adversarial context prevails, whether the decision under review will have some practical effect on the rights of the parties and will not be a waste of judicial resource, and finally, whether a judgment by the Court will be seen as an intrusion into the legislative branch of government.
