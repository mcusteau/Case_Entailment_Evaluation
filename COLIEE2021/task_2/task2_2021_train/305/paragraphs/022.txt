As the Federal Court of Appeal noted
Bristol-Myers Squibb Co. et al. v. Canada (Attorney General) et al.
(2003), 303 N.R. 63; 2003 FCA 180 (F.C.A.), at paragraph 13:
"This holistic approach to the interpretation of legislation [...] requires a court to attribute the meaning that provides the best fit with both the text and the context of the provision in question. Neither can be ignored, although the clearer the 'ordinary meaning' of the text, the more compelling the contextual considerations must be in order to warrant a different reading of it, especially when that involves adding words to those used by the legislator."
