Thus it would appear that if the disposition of the former property and the acquisition of the replacement property occur in the same year, the taxpayer need simply calculate the taxable capital gain on the basis that s. 44 applies and this, in my view, would constitute an election. It is only if the replacement property is acquired in a subsequent or prior year that, according to the bulletin, the taxpayer must expressly make a s. 44 election by attaching a letter to this effect in his or her tax return.
