  Turning to another issue, it is now clear, and accepted by the Attorney General, that ultimately, the four pronged test is a measure of fulfilling the underlying consideration, which is to ensure that justice is done between the parties (see  
 Hogervorst 
  at para. 33 and  
 Pentney 
  at para. 34). As such, an extension may be granted even if one of the factors mentioned in this test is not satisfied.
