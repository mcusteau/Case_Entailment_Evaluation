The appellant relies on the case of
Molson Cos. v. Mitches & Co. et al.
(1980), 50 C.P.R.(2d) 180 (F.C.T.D.), for the proposition that modifications to a mark should not alter the distinctive character of a mark, and upon
Buroughs-Wellcome v. Kirky, Shapiro, Eades & Cohen
(1983), 73 C.P.R.(2d) 13 (F.C.T.D.), for the proposition that use of different trademarks, even though sharing similar characteristics will not support a registration.
