  Summary judgment should be allowed where the court has evidence establishing the essential relevant facts such that proceeding to trial would add detail, but not significant additional evidence. See:  
 Pawar v. Canada 
 , [1999] 1 F.C. 158; 154 F.T.R. 271 (T.D.), affd. (1999), 247 N.R. 271 (F.C.A.), leave to appeal refused [1999] S.C.C.A. No. 526; 257 N.R. 398 (S.C.C.).
