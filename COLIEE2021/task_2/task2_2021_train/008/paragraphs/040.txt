  Without explicitly completing a state protection analysis, the Board referenced the existence of laws which define rape as a crime in Haiti. I note that there was documentary evidence which directly contradicted the Board's implicit assumption that state protection for people who fear violence in Haiti is adequate. The Report of the Secretary-General on the United Nations Stabilization Mission in Haiti and the Policy Briefing, authored by the International Crisis Group,  
 Haiti: Security and the Reintegration of the State 
 , Port-au-Prince/Brussels, 30 October 2006, explicitly discusses the instability of the state (as of 2006) and the specific incapacities of the state to police its citizens in a meaningful way.
