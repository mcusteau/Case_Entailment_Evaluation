In his 18 page reasons for decision, John Williams, Q.C., the presiding member of the C.R.D.D. found that the C.R.D.D. was empowered to address the issue of whether or not it had the jurisdiction to reopen a claim, but went on to hold that the C.R.D.D. could not reopen a matter where the sole purpose of the reopening would be the hearing of evidence of new facts.
