The result is a "sliding scale" of procedural safeguards necessitated by s. 7, depending on the context. In the words of Mr. Justice La Forest in
R. v. Lyons
, [1987] 2 S.C.R. 309; 80 N.R. 161; 82 N.S.R.(2d) 271; 207 A.P.R. 271; 37 C.C.C.(3d) 1; 61 C.R.(3d) 1, at 361:
"The requirements of fundamental justice are not immutable; rather they vary according to the context in which they are invoked. Thus, certain procedural protections might be constitutionally mandated in one context but not in another."
