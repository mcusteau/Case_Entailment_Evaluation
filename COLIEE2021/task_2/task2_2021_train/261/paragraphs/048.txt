Even if the applicants had requested a full oral hearing for humanitarian and compassionate consideration and had been turned down, their right to a "meaningful opportunity" to be heard would not have been violated. Section H(2)(f) of the same post-February 1993
Guidelines
provides:
"(f) Claimants not recognized as Convention refugees will have fifteen days following the determination of the C.R.D.D. within which to make submissions to an immigration officer, [R 11.4(3)]. The regulations provide this interval so the claimant (sic) can advance their arguments or representations on two issues: the extent of any risk they would face upon removal from Canada and whether that risk would exist in all parts of the country of removal."
