  In  
 Tsang v. Canada (Minister of Public Safety and Emergency Preparedness) 
 , [2006] F.C.J. No. 576; 290 F.T.R. 137; 2006 FC 474, Justice Dawson held that the scope of the parameters of the officer's delegated discretion to make a forfeiture decision was a question of law reviewable on the standard of correctness.
