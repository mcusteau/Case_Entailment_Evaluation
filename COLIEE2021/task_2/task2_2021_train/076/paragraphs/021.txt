  State protection does not have to be perfect. Even in countries where adequate state protection exists, authorities cannot guarantee protection of all of citizens at all times. The respondent submits that the Board was entitled to find that the Applicant had not rebutted the presumption of state protection. See  
 Kadenko v. Canada (Minister of Citizenship and Immigration) 
 , 143 D.L.R. (4th) 532 (F.C.A.).
