  However, the Minister may make an exception, since the Minister has the discretion to facilitate a person's admission to Canada or exempt a person from any criterion or obligation set out in the IRPA if the Minister is satisfied that this is justified by humanitarian and compassionate (H&C) considerations (subsection 25(1) of the IRPA;  
 Serda v. Canada 
   
 (Minister of Citizenship and Immigration), 
  2006 FC 356, at paragraph 20).
