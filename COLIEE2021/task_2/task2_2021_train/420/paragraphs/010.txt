While plaintiff's counsel argues that
Premier Mouton
is applicable to the case at bar, he concedes that s. 68 is worded more broadly than its predecessor s. 46(6). The words "or otherwise" have been added to s. 68 so that errors contemplated by the section are "whether by reason of mistake of fact or law or otherwise". However, plaintiff's counsel says that the words "or otherwise" qualify the word "mistake". He says there was no mistake of any kind in this case because the plaintiff had protested the payment of tax, and the addition of the words "or otherwise" have no bearing.
