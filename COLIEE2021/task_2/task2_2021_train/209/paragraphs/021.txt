  The Commission has been conferred a significant degree of deference in choosing its own processes and procedures, especially in fulfilling its administrative and screening functions under the Act. This Court and the Federal Court of Appeal have repeatedly stated that the investigation process is not intended to provide the full range of natural justice to a complainant and that there is no right to quasi-judicial procedures such as an oral hearing or cross-examination of witnesses (see  
 McConnell v. Canada (Canadian Human Rights Commission) 
 , 2004 FC 817, [2004] F.C.J. No. 1005 (QL) at paragraph 90, aff'd 2005 FCA 389).
