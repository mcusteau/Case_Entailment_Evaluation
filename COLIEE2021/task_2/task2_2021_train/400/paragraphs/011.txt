The respondent submits that the visa officer's decision to refuse to accept the applicant's application for an employment authorization under the FDM Program is a discretionary decision requiring the assessment of a number of diverse factors. The respondent submits that this exercise of statutory discretion is not subject to an extraordinary remedy pursuant to s. 18 of the
Federal Court Act
if it had been exercised bona fide, uninfluenced by irrelevant considerations and not arbitrarily or illegally even if the court would have exercised the discretion differently.
