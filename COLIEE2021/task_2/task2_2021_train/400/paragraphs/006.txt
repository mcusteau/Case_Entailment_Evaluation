The applicant then received a letter dated September 2, 1988 asking her to attend at the Commission for Canada in Hong Kong on September 9, 1988. At that time the officer told the applicant that her application was not accepted because she had violated a Hong Kong immigration rule. In her affidavit at para. 10, the applicant states that:
"Upon my attendance there, the officer told me that my application for permanent residence in Canada could not be accepted because I had violated a Hong Kong immigration rule. The officer said that although my contract is legal here according to the Hong Kong Immigration Department, it is illegal in the eyes of the officials of the Canadian High Commission."
