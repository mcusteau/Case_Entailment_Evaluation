In short, the Immigration Officer owed a duty of fairness to consider all relevant reports, to inform Gao of a negative assessment and to provide him an opportunity to counter it. Gao was entitled to expect, particularly in the wake of the March 1991 letter, that he would be informed of further developments and would have the opportunity to present his case, if necessary, before the final decision was made. Moreover, 16 months had elapsed between the Glendale report and the inadmissibility decision, with no intervening request by the Immigration Officer for further examination of Qi.
