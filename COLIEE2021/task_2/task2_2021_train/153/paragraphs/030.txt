  In discharging this burden, refugee claimants who are outside their country of nationality may demonstrate either that they are "unable" to obtain adequate state protection or that,  
 by reason of a well founded fear of persecution 
 , are unwilling to avail themselves of the protection of their home state. As stated in  
 Ward 
 , above, at para 49: 
 
 
 
 The distinction between these two branches of the "Convention Refugee" definition resides in the party's precluding resort to state protection: in the case of "inability", protection is denied to the claimant, whereas when the claimant is "unwilling", he or she opts not to approach the state by reason of his or her fear on an enumerated basis.
