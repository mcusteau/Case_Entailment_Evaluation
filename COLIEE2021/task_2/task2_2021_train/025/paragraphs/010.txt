  In  
 Dunsmuir v. New Brunswick 
 , 2008 SCC 9, [2008] S.C.J. No. 9 (QL), the Supreme Court of Canada held at paragraph 62 that the first step in conducting a standard of review analysis is to "ascertain whether the jurisprudence has already determined in a satisfactory manner the degree of [deference] to be accorded with regard to a particular category of question."
