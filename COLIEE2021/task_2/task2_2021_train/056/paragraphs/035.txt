  The requirements for establishing inadmissibility on security grounds are thus less stringent than the requirements for exclusion on grounds of violating international human rights. The latter requires complicity or knowing participation in the commission of a specific international crime, while the former does not require any complicity or knowing participation in an act of terrorism. Mr. Kanapathy fails to appreciate these distinctions when he argues that the Officer erred by failing to show that he actually incited a terrorist act during his employment with
