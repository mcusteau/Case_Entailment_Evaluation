  When asked by the Board why he had stayed with the AFRC until it was ousted from power, the applicant responded that he had had no choice. The panel did not accept this explanation. The Board found instead that "[b]y not extricating himself at the earliest possible opportunity, and by remaining with the AFRC from their inception until they were ousted from Freetown, the claimant willingly subscribed to the ideology of an organization that was 'principally directed to a limited brutal purpose.'"
