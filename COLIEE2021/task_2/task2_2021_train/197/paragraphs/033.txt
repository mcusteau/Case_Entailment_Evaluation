  The goal of the  
 Trade-marks Act 
  is to prevent confusion in the marketplace. The Supreme Court has reminded us that "the legal purpose of trade-marks continues (in terms of s. 2 of the  
 Trade-marks Act 
 , R.S.C. 1985, c. T-13) to be their use by the owner "to distinguish wares or services manufactured, sold, leased, hired or performed by him from those manufactured, sold, leased, hired or performed by others". It is a guarantee of origin and inferentially, an assurance to the consumer that the quality will be what he or she has come to associate with a particular trade-mark ..." ( 
 Mattel 
 , above, per Binnie J. at para 2).
