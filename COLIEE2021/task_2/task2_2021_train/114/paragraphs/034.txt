  The petitioner returns to the Luscher case which applied the "void-for-vagueness" principle to a legislated statute in these words (at page 85): 
 
 
 
 "... Uncertainty and vagueness are constitutional vices when they are used to restrain constitutionally-protected rights and freedoms. While there can never be absolute certainty, a limitation of a guaranteed right must be such as to allow a very high degree of predictability of the legal consequences."
