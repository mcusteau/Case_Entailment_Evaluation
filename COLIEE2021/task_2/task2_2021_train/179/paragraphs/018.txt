  The first question therefore, to be determined, is whether the Board erred in finding that, on the balance of probabilities, there was no serious possibility that the applicant would be persecuted in Mexico outside Mexico City. The Refugee Division took the view that, on the evidence before it, the threats facing the applicant were strictly local in nature, and that once outside the jurisdiction of the federal judicial police the applicant had no reason to fear that he or his family would be subject to further violence as a result of his union activity and other forms of resistance to the demands of corrupt officials.
