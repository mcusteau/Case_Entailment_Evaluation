  Six (6) years later immigration officials reviewed the H&C application and invited further submissions. These submissions included increased levels of establishment by virtue of the establishment of a trucking business, and most importantly, the evidence that the youngest son suffered from eczema which would be exacerbated by the hot Indian climate. The Applicants also explained that they had never had the opportunity to leave Canada voluntarily because the Indian government had not issued them new passports.
