Under section 3 of the
Trade-Marks Act
, R.S.C. 1985, c. T-13 (the
Act
), a trade-mark is deemed to have been adopted by a person when that person or his predecessor in title commenced to use it in Canada or to make it known in Canada or, if that person or his predecessor had not previously so used it or made it known, when that person or his predecessor filed an application for its registration in Canada. In the case at bar, according to the evidence in the record the plaintiff is owner of the trademark BIO-LAWN, registered as No. TMA566,511 on August 27, 2002. Additionally, the plaintiff and its predecessors in title have also used the trade-mark BIO-LAWN since July 1998 for the following wares and services:
(a) wares: fertilizers and natural ameliorants for lawns and trees;
(b) services: spreading these wares on contract (fertilizers and natural ameliorants for lawns and trees).
