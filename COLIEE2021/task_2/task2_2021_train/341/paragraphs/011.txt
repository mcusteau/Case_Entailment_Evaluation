Pursuant to rule 600(2) of the
Federal Court Rules
, s. 48 governs the manner in which actions against the Crown are to be commenced. Section 48 of the
Federal Court Act
provides:
"48(1) A proceeding against the Crown shall be instituted by filing in the Registry of the Court the original and two copies of a document that may be in the form set out in the schedule and by payment of a sum of two dollars as a filing fee.
"(2) The original and two copies of the originating documents may be filed as required by subsection (1) by being forwarded, together with a remittance for the filing fee, by registered mail addressed to 'The Registry, The Trial Division, The Federal Court of Canada, Ottawa, Canada'."
