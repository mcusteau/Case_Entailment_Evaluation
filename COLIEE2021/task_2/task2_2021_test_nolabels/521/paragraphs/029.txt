In any event, the Immigration Division can be very flexible about the evidence it considers. It is not bound by any legal or technical rules of evidence, and it may rely and base a decision on any evidence adduced in the proceedings that it considers credible or trustworthy in the circumstances (
IRPA
, paragraphs 173(c) and (d);
Thanaratnam v. Canada (Minister of Citizenship and Immigration)
, [2004] F.T.R. Uned. 166; 3 F.C. 301; 2004 FC 349, at paragraph 7).
