To succeed, therefore, the respondents must establish the failure of the Minister to adhere to and respect the standard of disclosure. Clearly, an applicant, on an ex parte application, must exercise utmost good faith and ensure full and frank disclosure to be successful. In
Deputy Minister of National Revenue v. Atchison
(1988), 89 D.T.C. 5088 (B.C.S.C.), Sheppard, L.J.S.C., allowed the taxpayer's application to have a jeopardy collection order set aside because the Minister had not made full disclosure with respect to the allegations in its ex parte application.
