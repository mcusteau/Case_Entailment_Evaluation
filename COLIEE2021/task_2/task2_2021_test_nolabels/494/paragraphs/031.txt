In evaluating the degree of resemblance between trademarks, a Court should not dissect and subject them to careful analyses. Rather, it should put itself in the position of a person who has a general recollection of one ot them, and then, upon seeing the other at a later date, determine whether such person is likely to think that they originate from the same source. (
SkyDome Corp. v. Toronto Heart Industries Ltd. et al.
(1997), 128 F.T.R. 71; 72 C.P.R.(3d) 546 at 549 (T.D.)). However, it is still possible to focus on particular features of the mark which may have a determinative influence on the public's perception of it. (
Pink Panther Beauty Corp.
, supra at 263).
